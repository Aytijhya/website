Research Interests and Activities

My research interests span a broad spectrum within theory, methodology, and applications of Statistics and Machine Learning. However, the most of my current research focus is centered around causal inference, sequential/anytime-valid inference, and distribution-free inference. Details of my publications and preprints can also be found in my homepage and google scholar profile.

Beyond research, I enjoy engaging with the broader scientific community through conferences, collaborations etc. I enjoy traveling to workshops and conferences around the world, where I present my work, exchange ideas with researchers from diverse backgrounds, and gain new perspectives that often inspire future research directions. I also like organizing academic events. I co-organized Sanjoy K. Mitter LIDS Student Conference in 2026.
